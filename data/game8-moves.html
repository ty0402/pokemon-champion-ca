Moves Deal Damage Based on Opposing Pokemon Type


Pokemon moves are divided into 18 distinct types, from the basic Fire, Water, and Grass types to the mighty Dragon-type.

Moves can do more or less damage depending on your Pokemon's move type and the opposing Pokemon's type, so make sure to use moves that attack an opposing Pokemon's weakness!

Moves Have Three Categories

As is with every Pokemon game, every move falls under one of three categories: Physical, Special, or Status.

Physical moves use the attacker’s Attack stat and the target’s Defense stat when calculating damage while Special moves use the attacker’s Special Attack stat and the target’s Special Defense stat. Status moves do not deal direct damage but instead provide buffs, debuffs, or inflict status conditions.

Some Moves Have A Secondary Effect


Certain moves have secondary effects that may activate during battle. These effects can include a chance to cause status conditions, lowering an opponent’s stats, or providing team support like setting up Reflect or Light Screen.

Secondary effects are not guaranteed unless the move specifically states otherwise.

How to Learn Moves

All Moves Can Be Learned via Training


To make it easier for players, Pokemon Champions now allows Trainers to quickly add any move a Pokemon is able to learn in Training Mode. No more fussing around with Heart Scales, TMs, and Breeding!

Move Changes in Pokemon Champions

Some Moves Have Been Readjusted

In Pokemon Champions, some moves have their base power, accuracy, and secondary effects changed. PP has also been standardized across the game and are automatically maxed out, removing the need to use PP Up or PP Max.

Additionally, some Pokemon have their learnset changed, with some getting new moves while others have moves removed.
