Bulky and Strong Physical Attacker

Kingambit is a very bulky Pokemon with a ton of Attack. They also have the Defiant ability, meaning that any time a stat is lowered, like an Attack drop from Intimidate, it will gain +2 Attack.

Defiant Effect and Pokemon With This Ability

Missing Items Weakens Potential

Unfortunately, a lot of its useful items, like Assault Vest and Safety Goggles, aren't in the game yet. This makes it susceptible to some of its common weaknesses, like special attacks and status effects.

Standard Bulky Kingambit Weaknesses

Counter Pokemon | Info
Target Its Low Sp. Def
While Kingambit's Sp. Def isn't that low, it's the lowest of its two defensive stats. Using special attacks while take it down a lot faster than physical moves, especially while Assault Vest isn't in the game yet.
Be Careful of Speed Control
Even if Kingambit is very slow, it can still outspeed a lot of Pokemon after Tailwind, Icy Wind, and especially after Trick Room. Make sure that you also have ways to control the Speed on your side of the field so that you don't have to deal with the absolute menace that's a fast Kingambit.

Kingambit Checks and Counters

Counter Pokemon | Info
Target Its Fighting Weakness
Besides Ground and Fire moves, Fighting is Kingambit's biggest weakness. Since it's a pretty common Pokemon, you'll want to make sure to have a Fighting-type counter ready, or at least a Pokemon with Low Kick to take advantage of its heavy weight.
Apply the Burn Status
Since you'll want to avoid using Intimidate to prevent Defiant Attack boosts, the best way to reduce Kingambit's damage is by burning it instead. This will halve their Attack stat, potentially letting allies live attacks they normally wouldn't be able to.

Exploit Its 4x Fighting Weakness

Since Terastallization isn't in the game yet, Kingambit has no way to avoid its crippling weakness to Fighting. Thankfully, there aren't a ton of strong Fighting options at the moment, but there are still a lot of powerful Fighting moves available to a lot of Pokemon.

List of All Fighting-Type Pokemon

Halve Attack Stat with Burns

Because of Defiant, it's a bad idea to lower its Attack stat with Intimidate since it will gain a net +1 Attack boost after the interaction ends. This means that the most consistent way to reduce its damage output is by Burning it, like with Will-O-Wisp, for example.
